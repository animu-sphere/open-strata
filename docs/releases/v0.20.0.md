# v0.20.0 - dogfood closure and renderer workflow

## Objective

v0.20.0 closes the package, product, renderer, and Formation gaps found by
the v0.19.0 downstream dogfoods. The release keeps the contracts
runtime-, package-, and renderer-native; DCC host integration is deferred to
v0.21.0.

## Completed capabilities

- **Independently installable package closure.** Packaged bundle dependencies
  carry their USD registration and link/runtime trees, declare them in the
  activation contract, and pass package-origin execution without a sibling
  source package.
- **Byte-reproducible aggregate products.** Product and member manifests use a
  deterministic timestamp contract, and repeated product creation retains the
  same aggregate digest across supported platforms.
- **Product verification and installation.** `ost plugin product verify`
  validates the outer product, members, checksums, evidence, inventory, and
  bundle contracts. `ost plugin product install` verifies before extracting a
  dependency-ordered product and emits portable activation entrypoints.
- **Actionable managed recovery.** Managed build and renderer timeout failures
  retain phase, process, command, cwd, log-tail, and cleanup evidence while
  releasing the target lease when the process is actually terminated.
- **Renderer workflow preflight and evidence closure.**
  `ost renderer viewport --preflight` validates intent, target, profile,
  scene capabilities, and passthrough workflow before a build. Managed
  renderer reports are bound to producer sessions and digests, and success and
  failure paths share one durable launch record and JSON shape.
- **Formation environment and diagnostics.** `ost formation env` exports the
  verified composed environment, while `ost formation doctor` reports
  resolution, lock freshness, environment conflicts, and command reachability.

## Compatibility notes

- DCC host discovery and adapters are not part of this release; they move to
  v0.21.0.
- Formation remains digest-pinned and package-backed. It does not introduce
  implicit downloads from untrusted sources or detached session management.
- Multi-config external imports require an explicit concrete `--config`.

## Evidence

- Lifecycle tests cover repeated aggregate packaging, product verification and
  installation, package-origin dependency closure, renderer preflight and
  evidence binding, timeout cleanup, and Formation `env|doctor` diagnostics.
- Downstream acceptance was exercised with a real CY2026 Windows OpenUSD
  runtime and the hdMerlin `viewport-usd` workflow, including a hidden-frame
  launch and durable launch record.
